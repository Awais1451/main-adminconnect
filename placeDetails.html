Park Guell

Park Guell is a renowned public park located in Barcelona, Spain. Designed by the prominent architect Antoni Gaudi, the park showcases his distinct artistic style and innovative approach to urban planning. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a major tourist attraction, drawing visitors from around the globe to explore its mesmerizing architecture and lush green spaces.

History

Commissioned by Eusebi Guell, a wealthy entrepreneur and Gaudi's patron, Park Guell was envisioned as a residential garden city project. The construction began in 1900 and was completed in 1914, though the original idea of building houses within the park was not fully realized. Instead, the park evolved into a public space with impressive architectural elements and stunning vistas of Barcelona.

Architectural Marvels

Park Guell boasts an array of architectural marvels, exemplifying Gaudi's distinctive organic style. The iconic multicolored mosaic salamander, commonly known as "El Drac," greets visitors at the entrance, serving as a symbol of the park's whimsical and imaginative design.
